Falcon 9

Falcon 9 is a EELV-class medium-lift partially reusable two-stage-to-orbit launch vehicle, powered by nine Merlin engines and burns cryogenic liquid oxygen and rocket-grade kerosene (RP-1) as propellants.

It evolved from versions v1.0 (2010–2013), v1.1 (2013–2016) to v1.2 Full Thrust (2015–present). Since the introduction of the Full Thrust version it became partially reusable - the first stage is capable of re-entering the atmosphere and landing vertically after separation.

Five rockets of the version 1.0 were launched from 2010-2013, 15 of verison 1.1 from 2013-2016. Fromhereon version "Full Thrust" took over and has since seen additional upgrades within the version, and is to this day the current ongoing version of Falcon.

Block 5 was introduced in may of 2018 and it features increased engine thrust, improved landing legs and other improvements on recovery and reuse.

Specifics

Specifics concerning the Falcon 9 rocket

Carry Capacity

Able to lift payloads of up to 22,800 kg (50,300 lb) to low earth orbit, 8,300 kg (18,300 lb) to geostationary transfer orbit when expended and 5,500 kg (12,100 lb) to geostationary transfer orbit when first stage is recovered.

Rating

Human-rated for transporting NASA astronauts to the ISS as part of NASA Commercial Crew Development program.

Certifications

Certified for both the american National Security Space Launch program and NASA Launch Services program as a "Category 3" - meaning it can launch the priciest, most important, and most complex of NASA missions.

As of early 2020, the Falcon 9 family has flown 84 successful missions

Falcon 1

Falcon 1 was the first launch system developed and manufactured by SpaceX in the period between 2006-2009 when it retired and was succeeded by Falcon 9.
It was a two-stage-to-orbit rocket that used LOX/RP-1 for both stages, the first powered by a single Merlin engine, the second were powered by a single Kestrel engine.

September 2008 the Falcon 1 made headlines by becoming the first privately-developed liquid-fuel launch vehicle to go into orbit around earth.

It would total up to five launches, the last flight took place july 2009 - it also marked the first commercial launch for SpaceX.

Falcon Heavy

The Falcon Heavy was introduced in early 2018, it consists of a strengthened Falcon 9 first stage as its center core and attached to two standard Falcon 9 first stages that are used as boosters. It has the highest payload capacity of any currently operational launch vehicle.

It's maiden launch took place in 2018 - it carried a red Tesla Roadster and a mannequin named Starman. Falcon Heavy has been certified for the american National Security Space Launch program.

Both Falcon Heavy and Falcon 9 will be replaced by the Starship launch system in the future.
