Successful launch of NASA astronauts

At Kennedy Space Center, for the first time since 2011, humans were blasted off into orbit from US soil. They were carried by a Falcon 9 rocket in a Dragon Crew capsule.

The spacecraft, named Endeavour, launched on 30 May 2020 at 19:22:45 UTC (3:22:45 PM EST) and carried NASA astronauts Douglas Hurley and Robert Behnken to the International Space Station.
Endeavour returned the astronauts to Earth on 02.08.2020 in the first water landing by astronauts since 1975.

Reusable Launch-system

reusable rockets for the future

The reusable launcher program was announced back in 2011 and the design finished in 2012. It is capable of returning the first stage of a Falcon 9 rocket to a predetermined landing site using only its own propulsion systems.High-velocity, high-altitude aspects of the booster atmospheric return technology began testing in late 2013 and have continued through 2018, with a 98% success rate to date.

In march 2017 a Falcon 9 launched for the SES-10 mission - making it the first time a re-launch of a payload-carrying orbital rocket went back to space. The first stage was recovered and landed on the ASDS "Of Course I Still Love You", also making it the first landing of a reused orbital class rocket.

Arrival of the dragon

Dragon was annouced back in 2005 together with plans to persue a human-rated commercial space program. Dragon is a conventional blunt-cone ballistic capsule that is capable of carrying cargo or up to seven astronauts into orbit and beyond.

In 2010 an operational Dragon was launched on a Falcon 9 rocket, it safely returned to Earth after two orbits, completing all its mission objectives.
