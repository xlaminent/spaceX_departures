The creation of the Dragon

Spacecrafts

Dragon 1

Dragon 1, or Cargo Dragon, was the resulting reusable cargo spacecraft born of plans that aimed to pursue a human-rated commercial space program since 2005. A year later NASA would announce that SpaceX would be selected to provide cargo, and crew, resupply demonstration contracts to the ISS under the COTS (Commercial Orbital Transportation Services) - program.

In 2010 an operational Dragon spacecraft would be launched aboard COTS Demo Flight 1, it returned to earth after two orbits and with all it's mission objectives completed.

Two years later Dragon 1 became the first commercial spacecraft to deliver cargo to ISS - and has since been conducting regular resupply services.

Dragon 1 had it last launch march 2020 as it finished the last mission for the first Commercial Resupply Services-1 program.

Dragon 2

Dragon 2 is developed as the successor to Dragon 1, and unlike it has two variants;

Crew Dragon - space capsule capable of carrying up to seven astronauts.
Cargo Dragon - updated replacement for the original Dragon 1.

Both variants are launched atop a Falcon 9 Block 5 rocket and returns to earth via an ocean-splashdown.
Unlike Dragon 1 it can dock itself to the ISS once in orbit instead of being berthed - a non-autonomous means to attach that was completed by use of the Canadarm2 robotic arm. The spacecraft also has exclusive features as the redesigned solar arrays, a modified outer mold line and it possesses a new flight computer

Future resupply flights to the ISS under the second Commercial Resupply Services-2 program will use Cargo Dragon. It's first flight is planned to launch in october 2020.

Commercial Crew Program

SpaceX became a part of NASA's Commercial Crew Program in 2014 when chosen to develop systems that would transport U.S crews to and from ISS.

In early 2019, a successfull test flight was conducted of Crew Dragon where it completed the spacecraft's first mission to the International Space Station. Later the capsule splashed down just off the Florida coast in the Atlantic Ocean.

On may 30th, 2020, astronauts Bob Behnken and Doug Hurley were launched to the ISS with a Crew Dragon Endeavour as part of Crew Dragon Demo-2 mission.
This was the first crewed mission launched from US since 2011, and also the first commercially crewed ISS delivery.
Their safe return to earth happened on august 2 - it also marked the first splashdown in 45 years for NASA astronauts - as well as the first splashdown of a crew spacecraft in the Gulf of Mexico.
